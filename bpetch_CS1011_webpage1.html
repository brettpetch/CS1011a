Bradford District High School

Bradford District High School is located in Bradford, Ontario: a small town of around 36,000. Bradford District High School is a public secondary school located in Bradford, Ontario. It is part of the Simcoe County District School Board. The principal is Peter Stone and vice principals Graham Rayner & Karen Quesnel. During the last half of the 2006-2007 school year BDHS underwent extensive renovations that included a new greenhouse and full expansion and renovation of existing facilities. In 2017, the school underwent another renovation, where (because of compliance issues) the school received a government grant for a new elevator.

In Fall 2017, Bradford District High School was given another government grant for an experiment of sorts, through the TLLP program. The author of this web page was involved in the creation of a video, and it has been linked on the details portion of this website.

Notable People

Bradford District High School’s students and graduates have gone on to shine in their professions.

  • Frank Ferragine (class of 1990) is a television personality on CityTV’s Breakfast Television.
  • Nicole Forrester (class of 1994) is a 7-time national high jump champion, a Commonwealth Games gold medallist, and represented Canada at the 2008 Olympic Games.
  • Dr. Ryan Van Lieshout (class of 1996) is the Albert Einstein/Irving Zucker Chair in Neuroscience at McMaster University.
  • Brandon Mashinter(class of 2006) played in the NHL for the San Jose Sharks, New York Rangers and the Chicago Blackhawks.
  • Dr. Tyler Beveridge (class of 2007) is a cancer researcher in the Department of Anatomy and Cell Biology at the University of Western Ontario.
